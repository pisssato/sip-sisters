Beer selection of the month

Ecliptic Capella Porter
Style: Porter
ABV: 5.2%
Brewery origin: Portland, Oregon
About the beer:
“Maybe it’s a stretch to call a beer that has won gold at [the] World Beer Cup and the Oregon Beer Awards underrated, but this is the kind of beer that often gets overlooked in today’s landscape where raves are reserved for bombastic pastry stouts, hazies, and crispy lagers."
Pilsner Urquell
Style: Czech Pilsner
ABV: 4.4%
Brewery origin: Plzen, Czech Republic
About the beer:
“It’s refreshing and crisp, yet has enough of a malt backbone to keep it interesting. The grassy, floral hop aroma is inviting but doesn’t dull the senses as many hoppy beers are likely to do. Amazingly it makes it to the U.S. in pretty good shape, too!”
North Coast Tart Cherry Berliner Weiss
Style: Berliner Weiss
ABV: 4.1%
Brewery origin: Fort Bragg, California
About the beer:
“They blend in Montmorency cherry juice which gives it a balanced acidity, elicate aroma and a beautiful color. It is low in alcohol, very sessionable and delicious; three of the things I always look for in a beer.”
Mayflower Porter
Style: Porter
ABV: 5.2%
Brewery origin: Plymouth, Massachusetts
About the beer:
“It’s refreshing and crisp, yet has enough of a malt backbone to keep it interesting. The grassy, floral hop aroma is inviting but doesn’t dull the senses as many hoppy beers are likely to do. Amazingly it makes it to the U.S. in pretty good shape, too!”
Birra Moretti La Rossa
Style: Doppelbock
ABV: 7.2%
Brewery origin: San Giorgio di Nogaro, Italy
About the beer:
“While regular Birra Moretti is a relatively bland industrialized lager, the La Rossa is a wonderful German-style doppelbock made in Italy. Clear, malty, and bitter enough to balance the sweetness."
Fullers London Pride
Style: English Bitter
ABV: 4.1%
Brewery origin: London, England
About the beer:
“I was born close to the brewery and worked for many years close to it, driving past it every day. It exudes London—ester forward British yeast, smooth British caramel malt, and earthy hops. Such a sessionable beer, born in (parti-gyle) tradition.” — Adam Robbings, Reuben’s Brews
Aecht Schlenkerla Rauchbier
Style: Rauchbier
ABV: 5.1%
Brewery origin: Bamberg, Germany
About the beer:
“I think a largely unappreciated category of beer is smoked beer. And of course, the high water mark for the category has got to be Schlenkerla. I was fortunate enough to visit them at the source in Bamberg this past summer. And it was lovely to see dozens of locals drinking it as their local beer… like no big deal. Smoke and all.” — Scott Smith, East End Brewing Company
Sierra Nevada Pale Ale
Style: Pale Ale
ABV: 5.6%
Brewery origin: Chico, California
About the beer:
Sierra Nevada Pale Ale was the beer that opened my eyes to the magic of hops back in the ‘90s. These guys have been pounding out liquid forever and still put out the same great beer day after day. Everyone should have one every now and then, if for no other reason than as a way to say thanks to Mr. Grossman.” — Mike Halker
Brasserie Dupont Saison Dupont
Style: Saison
ABV: 6.5%
Brewery origin: Leuze-en-Hainaut, Belgium
About the beer:
“Saison Dupont, a beer born before the current era of fervent rating culture, generally available, packaged in a bottle and a style that generates as much chatter as… things that generate no chatter. It’s wonderfully drinkable, dry and lightly funky, shining with its namesake yeast character and green bottle must. It’s effervescent, crisp and just tasty.”
North Coast Old Stock Ale
Style: Old Ale
ABV: 10.2%
Brewery origin: Fort Bragg, California
About the beer:
t’s a bottle shop sleeper, and becomes something really special after aging for a few years. I’ve been picking some up annually since I’ve been a brewer, and it’s always fun to crack a dusty one from the back of the cellar.” — Seth Morton
